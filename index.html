12-Week CrossFit Improvement Plan

Focus: pacing, engine, and body-composition-friendly training structure

Core weekly structure

  • Keep 5 CrossFit classes each week
  • Add 2–3 Zone 2 sessions
  • Add 1 interval session
  • Add 1 pacing-focus day each week
  • Add 1 engine builder and 2 clean-efficiency sessions

Main rules

  • First half of hard workouts at 70–75% effort
  • Do not bank time early
  • Zone 2 should feel conversational
  • Intervals should finish controlled, not trashed
  • Consistency beats hero days

What success looks like

  • Better pacing on long workouts
  • Less dramatic fade after minute 5–6
  • More consistent barbell work under fatigue
  • Improved 2k row capacity over the cycle
Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4 · Base Build
Build aerobic base and rhythm without redlining everything.
Zone 2: 2–3 sessions of 30–35 min at easy conversational pace
Intervals: 5 × 400 m row, 2 min rest, controlled pace
Engine builder: 12 min steady of 10 burpees + 8 cleans + 8 thrusters
Clean work: 2 sessions of EMOM 10 min, 1–2 moderate cleans
Pacing cue: deliberately finish workouts stronger than you start
Phase 2 · Weeks 5–8 · Capacity Build
Increase work density while staying controlled.
Zone 2: 2–3 sessions of 35–45 min
Intervals: 6 × 500 m row, 90 sec rest
Engine builder: 15 min steady of 12 burpees + 10 cleans + 10 thrusters
Clean work: same EMOM structure, slightly sharper execution
Pacing cue: first round is setup, not a race
Phase 3 · Weeks 9–12 · Endurance & Control
Turn the engine into repeatable performance.
Zone 2: 2–3 sessions of 45–50 min
Intervals: 5 × 600 m row, 90 sec rest
Engine builder: 18 min steady of 12 burpees + 12 cleans + 12 thrusters
Clean work: calm fast singles under fatigue
Pacing cue: hunt people down late instead of dying early

Nutrition Strategy for Performance, Fat Loss & Real Life

Simple rules for cafeteria life, weekends, restaurants, and quick recovery after off-plan meals

Core strategy

  • Protein at every meal
  • Visual portions instead of calorie tracking
  • Protein + veggies first, then choose one extra
  • No invisible snacking
  • Next meal resets everything

Cafeteria defaults

  • Breakfast: eggs, omelette, Greek yogurt, fruit
  • Lunch: half plate protein + veg, quarter carbs
  • Dinner: protein + vegetables, carbs as needed
  • Prefer grilled/simple meals over creamy/fried mixed dishes

Weekend rules

  • 1–2 drinks can fit
  • Choose one indulgence per meal: fries, dessert, or drinks
  • Plate your snacks instead of grazing
  • Walk, hydrate, and reset fast after big meals
Restaurant defaults
Domino’s: thin crust, protein/veg toppings, about 2–3 slices
Bella Alice: protein-focused entrée + veg, or stop around 75% on pasta
Confucius Dumplings: about 10–12 dumplings plus vegetables or soup
Damage control
Next meal: protein + vegetables
Hydrate hard after salty food or alcohol
Take a 20–40 minute walk
Do not punish yourself with fasting or revenge workouts
Use the next 3 meals to restore structure

2-Week Pull-Up Improvement Program

Built for an athlete with about 18 strict unbroken pull-ups

Main focus

  • Submax volume
  • Strength via weighted or tempo work
  • Density and repeatability
  • Grip endurance

Main rules

  • Keep most sets around 60–75% of max
  • Do not hit failure until test day
  • Most sets should feel clean and repeatable
Week 1
Day 1: 5 rounds of 6 pull-ups, rest, 8 pull-ups
Day 2: 5 × 5 weighted or tempo pull-ups + dead hangs
Day 3: 12-min EMOM of 5 reps, progress to some 6s if clean
Day 4: scap pull-ups, rows, hollow hold
Day 5: 5 × 10 pull-ups or 4 × 12
Day 6: dead hangs and top-position holds
Day 7: rest
Week 2
Day 8: 6 rounds of 8 pull-ups
Day 9: 6 × 4 weighted or paused pull-ups
Day 10: 14-min EMOM of 5s, then some 6s if clean
Day 11: light structural day
Day 12: 4 sets of 8–10 crisp reps
Day 13: max strict set + 3 back-off sets
Day 14: rest

2-Week Double-Under Endurance Program

Built for an athlete who can hit about 50 unbroken once, but wants repeatability

Main focus

  • Turn one good set into multiple good sets
  • Improve breathing and rhythm
  • Reduce misses after fatigue starts

Main rules

  • Most sets live in the 20–40 rep range
  • Stay relaxed in shoulders and grip
  • After a miss, reset calmly instead of panic-rushing
Week 1
Day 1: 8 rounds of 25 double unders
Day 2: singles + doubles rhythm practice
Day 3: 12-min EMOM alternating 20 and 25
Day 4: rest or easy movement
Day 5: 5 rounds of 40 reps, capped at 2 attempts each round
Day 6: fatigue rhythm practice with simple bodyweight work
Day 7: rest
Week 2
Day 8: 6 rounds of 30–35 reps
Day 9: singles + 25 doubles for 10 rounds
Day 10: 14-min EMOM alternating 25 and 30
Day 11: rest or easy movement
Day 12: 5 sets of 25–30 clean reps
Day 13: max test + 3 follow-up quality sets
Day 14: rest
